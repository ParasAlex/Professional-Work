Your Natural Tendencies

Based on your responses to our assessment questions, we have determined how you compare to others on five factors measuring natural tendencies. These tendencies are neither good nor bad, but they describe some likely behaviors and give insight into how you may interact with others on the job.

Your top Natural Tendency

Highly Composed

Patient
Confident
Even-Tempered
Being highly composed, you are likely to:
  • Be confident in your abilities
  • Feel more relaxed than others when confronted with obstacles
  • Be comfortable accepting constructive feedback from others
At work, as someone who is highly composed, you might:
  • Be patient and easy-going with others
  • Approach work in a relaxed and confident manner
  • Be more likely to remain calm under pressure
Possible growth opportunities:
  • Evaluate when tasks might require a more urgent response
  • Take time to consider the potential risks of an idea or plan

Your top Natural Tendency

Highly Helpful

Agreeable
Personable
Customer-Oriented
Being highly helpful, you are likely to:
  • Be helpful and responsive to the needs of others
  • Take others' feelings into consideration before acting
  • Respect and trust others
At work, as someone who is highly helpful, you might:
  • Go out of your way to assist coworkers and customers
  • Be sincere and pleasant when interacting with others
  • Accommodate the needs of others
Possible growth opportunities:
  • Remember to remain objective when it comes to the value of your time
  • Don’t shy away from disagreeing when necessary

Your top Natural Tendency

Highly Outgoing

Outspoken
Assertive
Enthusiastic
Being highly outgoing, you are likely to:
  • Be more sociable and talkative than others
  • Enjoy meeting new people and initiating conversations
  • Take charge of social interactions
At work, as someone who is highly outgoing, you might:
  • Seek out opportunities to interact with others
  • Appreciate receiving public forms of recognition
  • Be more comfortable leading or facilitating discussions than others
Possible growth opportunities:
  • Provide a space for more reserved coworkers to be heard and praised
  • Take time to recognize the achievements of others

Your top Natural Tendency

Moderately Open-Minded

Inquisitive
Innovative
Original
Being moderately open-minded, you are likely to:
  • Be interested in exploring new ways of performing work
  • Enjoy solving difficult or abstract problems
  • Be intellectually curious and seek out information on many subjects
At work, as someone who is moderately open-minded, you might:
  • Challenge organizational traditions or ways of performing tasks
  • Suggest unconventional or innovative ideas
  • Attempt to understand the root of a problem
Possible growth opportunities:
  • Spend time evaluating if a suggestion or idea is practical
  • Consider that existing procedures may be more efficient than new ones

Your top Natural Tendency

Moderately Spontaneous

Flexible
Casual
Bold
Being moderately spontaneous, you are likely to:
  • Be comfortable in less organized environments than others
  • Make quick or spur of the moment decisions
  • Have a more relaxed and casual pace
At work, as someone who is moderately spontaneous, you might:
  • Have a more flexible and unstructured approach to work
  • Be comfortable performing shorter, more varied tasks
  • Prefer tasks that require less attention to detail
Possible growth opportunities:
  • Take time to build a list of priorities before beginning new projects
  • Evaluate when tasks require more organization or attention to detail
